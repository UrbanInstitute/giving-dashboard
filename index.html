Generosity Dashboard

Frebruary XX, 2022

How Americans give has constantly changed over the decades, reflecting new social, economic, cultural, and technologic trends. Although certain trends in charitable giving have remained remarkably stable over the last half century—giving to 501(c)(3) tax-exempt charities as a proportion of individual disposable income has stayed near 2 percent for forty years, for instance—others have undergone significant shifts. Donor-advised funds, online crowdfunding, and digital giving have emerged and gained in prominence in recent decades, transforming generosity and the ways researchers define, track, and measure giving.

Below, we have collected and organized data to provide a snapshot of today’s generosity to charitable organizations and beyond. We define generosity expansively, going beyond giving to tax-exempt non-profits to include crowd-funding, impact investing, and political contributions. Each figure in this dashboard represents an indicator of generosity that, when viewed together, illuminate the dominant trends shaping the giving landscape. We recognize that many types of generosity resist quantification, but we hope this dashboard can provide a window into the multifaceted state of generosity in the US.

About the Data

This dashboard synthesizes the most comprehensive, significant, and recent data available on giving from leading nonprofit researchers, practitioners and service providers, as well as from some governmental sources. We conducted a landscape scan of giving data in the field and selected our indicators based on a set of core criteria: analytic rigor, salience, timeliness, and longitudinal availability.

For more information on data sources, select an indicator to find a short summary on how it was calculated. If you are interested in more detailed methodological information, click on the indicator’s original source to dive deeper into the data.
